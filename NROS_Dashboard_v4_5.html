The Samsara Method

The Samsara Method is a comprehensive analytical framework for assessing the health, resilience, and trajectory of human-made systems at any scale. It is built on the Six Pillars of civilizational function (Energy, Information, Product, Service, Transport, Entertainment), the Seven Axioms that govern their interaction, and the Eight-Step analytical process that produces actionable intelligence.

The Eight Steps

Step 1 — Pillar Mapping: Compute Pillar Health Index (PHI) for each of six Pillars from weighted sub-indicators using seven normalization methods.

Step 2 — Gradient Analysis: Assess inter-Pillar gradient spread and user-defined gradients. Determine gradient regime via Hodge decomposition.

Step 3 — Polity Assessment: Evaluate four failure modes (Mismanagement, Malfeasance, Rent-Seeking, Incompetence) and compound combinations.

Step 4 — SOC Assessment: Measure stored energy across five categories. Derive criticality indicators τ, σ, ρ.

Step 5 — Conflict Assessment: Apply Clausewitz's Remarkable Trinity (Passion, Chance, Purpose) across five domains with escalation and termination analysis.

Step 6 — Quantification: Hodge Laplacian decomposition on K₆ Pillar graph separating gradient, curl, and harmonic components. Cascade risk computation via 6×6 interdependency matrix.

Step 7 — Synthesis: Multi-factor trajectory projection producing probability-weighted scenarios. Narrative assessment generation.

Step 8 — Recursive Refinement: Consistency checks between cascade risks, polity failure, and threshold status. Output TreatiseContext for Hologram2 ingestion.

Seven Pillar Axioms

1. Every system depends on all six Pillars. 2. Energy is the primary enabler. 3. Disruption cascades via the interdependency matrix. 4. Energy disruptions receive priority weighting. 5. System resilience = min(PHI), not average. 6. Four or more stressed Pillars triggers cascading failure. 7. All Pillars are contestable.

Normalization Methods

Direct: min(raw/bench, 1). Inverted: max(0, 1−raw/bench). Z-Score: Φ(−|z|)×2. HHI: max(0, 1−raw). Grade: A=1…F=0. Percent: raw/100. Sigmoid: 1/(1+e^((raw/100−bench/100)/0.15)).

Hologram2 Integration

This dashboard embeds the complete NROS engine from Hologram2 v2.0. The TreatiseContext output (View 8) is fully compatible with Hologram2's Python environment. PHI scores feed Subsystem 1 (Hodge) and Subsystem 4 (Criticality). Gradient spread maps to inequality_gradient. SOC criticality contextualizes Subsystem 8 (Geopolitical Shock). The K₆ Hodge decomposition runs natively in-browser — no Python bridge required.

Interdependency Matrix

Energy→Product: 0.95 (Haber-Bosch). Energy→Transport: 0.90. Information→Service: 0.90. Energy→Information: 0.85. Information→Entertainment: 0.85. Service→Entertainment: 0.75. All 30 directed couplings are used in cascade risk computation.
